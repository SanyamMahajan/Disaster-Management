TORNADO PREPARATION



Tornadoes are violent windstorms characterized by a twisting, funnel-shaped cloud. They can cause extensive damage and pose significant risks to life and property. Tornadoes can:
  • Develop rapidly, giving little time to react.
  • Produce winds exceeding 300 miles per hour.
  • Cause destruction along their path, uprooting trees and demolishing buildings.

IF YOU ARE UNDER A TORNADO WARNING:

  • Seek shelter immediately in a sturdy building, preferably in a basement or small, windowless interior room.
  • Stay away from windows and doors to avoid flying debris.
  • Listen to local weather updates and follow the instructions of emergency officials.

HOW TO STAY SAFE WHEN A TORNADO THREATENS

Prepare NOW

  • Create an emergency plan that includes a designated safe location for your family during a tornado.
  • Prepare a disaster kit with essential supplies, including food, water, and a flashlight.
  • Know the signs of a tornado, such as a dark, greenish sky or a loud roar, similar to a freight train.
  • Sign up for local alerts and monitor weather forecasts during tornado season.

Survive DURING

  • Move to your designated safe area immediately when a tornado warning is issued.
  • If you are in a mobile home, abandon it and seek shelter in a nearby building or designated storm shelter.
  • Cover yourself with a mattress or heavy blankets to protect against debris.

Be Safe AFTER

  • Stay tuned to local news for updates on the situation and recovery efforts.
  • Avoid entering damaged buildings until officials have deemed them safe.
  • Be cautious of downed power lines and report them to the authorities.
  • Help neighbors and others who may need assistance.
  • Document any damage to your property for insurance purposes.
