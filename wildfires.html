WILDFIRE PREPARATION



Wildfires are uncontrolled fires that burn in forests, grasslands, or other natural areas. They can be caused by human activity or natural events such as lightning. Wildfires can:
  • Destroy homes, wildlife habitats, and natural resources.
  • Pollute air quality with smoke and ash.
  • Endanger lives and cause evacuations.

IF YOU ARE UNDER A WILDFIRE WARNING:

  • Evacuate immediately if authorities advise you to do so.
  • Follow evacuation routes as directed by local officials.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and listen for emergency alerts.

HOW TO STAY SAFE WHEN A WILDFIRE THREATENS

Prepare NOW

  • Know your evacuation routes and have a plan for your family.
  • Create a defensible space around your home by clearing flammable materials.
  • Have an emergency kit ready with food, water, medications, and important documents.
  • Sign up for local emergency alerts to receive warnings about wildfires.

Survive DURING

  • If a wildfire threatens your home, evacuate immediately.
  • Close all windows and doors, and turn off vents to reduce smoke infiltration.
  • Stay indoors and keep your pets indoors as well.
  • Use air conditioning if available to filter out smoke.

Be Safe AFTER

  • Listen to local news for updates on wildfire conditions and safety.
  • Avoid driving through smoke, as visibility may be reduced.
  • Be cautious of damaged structures and downed power lines.
  • Check on neighbors, especially the elderly or those with disabilities.
  • Document any property damage for insurance claims.
