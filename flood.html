FLOOD PREPARATION



Flooding is a natural disaster that can occur due to heavy rain, storms, or melting snow. Floods can:
  • Damage property and infrastructure.
  • Displace people and animals.
  • Contaminate water supplies and pose health risks.

IF YOU ARE UNDER A FLOOD WARNING:

  • Evacuate if advised by local authorities and move to higher ground.
  • Do not attempt to drive through flooded roads; turn around and find an alternate route.
  • Stay away from rivers, streams, and creeks that can rise quickly.

HOW TO STAY SAFE WHEN A FLOOD THREATENS

Prepare NOW

  • Know your areaâ€™s flood risk and evacuation routes.
  • Have an emergency kit prepared with food, water, medical supplies, and important documents.
  • Sign up for local alerts to receive warnings about impending floods.
  • Identify safe places to go in case of evacuation.

Survive DURING

  • If you are instructed to evacuate, do so immediately.
  • Move to higher ground and avoid walking through floodwaters.
  • Stay indoors if you cannot evacuate and keep away from windows and doors.

Be Safe AFTER

  • Stay tuned to local news and emergency services for updates on flooding and safety.
  • Avoid entering floodwaters, as they may be contaminated or contain hidden hazards.
  • Check for gas leaks and report any suspicious odors to authorities.
  • Document property damage with photographs for insurance claims.
  • Use text messages or social media to communicate with family and friends to avoid overloading phone systems.
