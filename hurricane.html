HURRICANE PREPARATION



Hurricanes are powerful storms that can cause severe damage to coastal areas and inland regions. They are characterized by strong winds, heavy rain, and storm surges. Hurricanes can:
  • Cause flooding and significant property damage.
  • Disrupt power and communication lines.
  • Endanger lives and displace communities.

IF YOU ARE UNDER A HURRICANE WARNING:

  • Evacuate if advised to do so by local authorities.
  • Secure your home and prepare for potential power outages.
  • Stay tuned to local news and weather updates for the latest information.

HOW TO STAY SAFE WHEN A HURRICANE THREATENS

Prepare NOW

  • Create an emergency kit with food, water, medications, and important documents.
  • Know your evacuation routes and have a plan for your family.
  • Install storm shutters or board up windows to protect against wind damage.
  • Sign up for local emergency alerts to receive warnings about hurricanes.

Survive DURING

  • Stay indoors during the storm and away from windows and doors.
  • Do not go outside until officials declare it safe to do so.
  • If flooding occurs, move to higher ground immediately.
  • Use flashlights instead of candles to avoid fire hazards.

Be Safe AFTER

  • Listen to local news for updates on recovery efforts and safety information.
  • Avoid driving through flooded areas, as water may be deeper than it appears.
  • Be cautious of downed power lines and report them to authorities.
  • Check on neighbors, especially the elderly or those with disabilities.
  • Document any property damage for insurance claims.
